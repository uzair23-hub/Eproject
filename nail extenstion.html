Nail Extenstion

Nail extensions are artificial enhancements that add length and shape to natural nails, giving them a polished and stylish appearance. They are perfect for individuals who want long, durable, and well-shaped nails without waiting for natural growth.



There are various types of nail extensions, including acrylic, gel, polygel, dip powder, and sculpted extensions, each offering unique benefits. Depending on the material and technique, nail extensions can last 2 to 6 weeks with proper care.
Extensions come in different shapes (coffin, almond, square, stiletto, oval, round) and can be customized with nail art, glitter, rhinestones, and designs for a unique and glamorous look.

Acrylic Nail

Acrylic nail extensions are artificial enhancements made by combining a liquid monomer and a powder polymer, which hardens when exposed to air. This creates a durable and long-lasting nail extension that can be shaped, filed, and customized with nail polish or nail art.


How Are Acrylic Nails Applied?
Nail Preparation: The natural nails are cleaned, shaped, and buffed to remove oils.
Tip Application (Optional): If extra length is needed, nail tips are glued onto the natural nail.
Acrylic Mixture Application: A brush is dipped into the liquid monomer and then into the powder polymer to create a bead, which is applied to the nail.
Shaping & Drying: The acrylic is sculpted into the desired shape and left to harden naturally.
Filing & Polishing: Once hardened, the nails are filed for smoothness and coated with polish or nail art.

Gel Nail Extenstion

Gel nail extensions are artificial nail enhancements created using gel-based formulas that are cured under a UV or LED lamp. They provide a lightweight, natural-looking, and flexible alternative to acrylic nails, making them ideal for those who want strong, yet less damaging nail extensions.


How Are Gel Nail Extensions Applied?
Nail Preparation: The natural nails are cleaned, shaped, and buffed to remove excess oil and enhance adhesion.
Tip or Form Application: A nail tip or sculpting form is used to extend the length of the nails.
Gel Application: A builder gel or hard gel is applied in layers and sculpted to the desired shape.
Curing Process: Each layer of gel is cured under a UV or LED lamp for 30-60 seconds to harden.
Shaping & Finishing: Once the gel is fully cured, the nails are filed, shaped, and polished for a sleek, smooth finish.
Top Coat & Final Cure: A final layer of gel top coat is applied and cured to seal the nails and add shine

Dip Powder Nail Extensions

Dip powder nail extensions are a durable and lightweight nail enhancement technique that combines the strength of acrylic with the smooth finish of gel. Instead of using UV light to cure the nails, this method involves dipping the nails into colored powder and sealing it with an activator, making it a healthier and long-lasting option. Dip powder nails are perfect for those who want strong, chip-resistant nails without the heavy feel of acrylics or the curing process of gels.


How Are Dip Powder Nail Extensions Applied?
Nail Preparation: The natural nails are cleaned, shaped, and buffed to remove excess oil and improve adhesion.
Base Coat Application: A bonding liquid or base coat is applied to help the powder stick.
Dipping Process: Nails are dipped into colored acrylic powder or the powder is sprinkled over the nail.
Layering: The process is repeated 2-3 times to build up strength and opacity.
Activator Application: A special activator liquid is applied to harden the powder and bond it to the nail.
Shaping & Buffing: Once hardened, nails are filed and shaped for a smooth finish.
Top Coat Application: A final glossy or matte top coat is applied to seal and protect the nails.
